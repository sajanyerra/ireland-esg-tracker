🔍 Key Findings

🔴 Ireland Off Track For 2030

Current emissions of 53.75 Mt must fall to 30.13 Mt by 2030 - requiring a 3.5x acceleration in the current pace of reduction in just 6 years.

🐄 Agriculture - Zero Progress

Agricultural emissions in 2024 (20.41 Mt) are virtually identical to 1990 levels (20.57 Mt). Agriculture now accounts for 37.97% of total emissions - nearly 4x the EU average.

🌱 Renewables Growing Too Slowly

Despite 387% growth since 2005, renewables reach only 14.6% of total energy vs a 43% target by 2030. Ireland is growing renewables 6.7x too slowly!

🖥️ Data Centre Challenge

Services sector energy consumption grew 51% (2005-2024) driven by Big Tech data centres. Ireland uses more electricity on data centres than on all urban homes combined.

📉 COVID Bounce Warning

The 2021 post-COVID emissions rebound wiped out two years of progress in one year - highlighting the need for structural rather than behavioural change.

🤖 ML Forecast: Miss Both Targets

Machine learning models forecast Ireland will reach 52.49 Mt emissions and 19.39% renewable energy by 2030 - missing both Climate Action Plan targets.
